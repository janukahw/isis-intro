Life of an experiment

Beam time is one of the scarcest resources in science: ~1,200 experiments a year, allocated by peer review, scheduled months ahead, and over in a few days. Here’s the whole journey — and where the software you build or run fits into it.

3.2 — Why an hour matters

A day on an instrument

ISIS runs in cycles — stretches of several weeks with beam on around the clock, separated by maintenance shutdowns. During a cycle, an instrument is handed from one user team to the next with barely a gap; experiments run through the night with support staff on call.

A typical allocation is two to five days. There are no do-overs: if equipment, beam, or software loses six hours, that might be a quarter of someone’s experiment — possibly one they waited eight months for. It’s the closest science gets to a live production deployment.
